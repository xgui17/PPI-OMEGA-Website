Model Performance Analysis

Our results demonstrate that integrating both RNA and protein expression features led to the highest performance, with an AUROC of 0.9115 and an AP of 0.9198. This confirms that incorporating multi-omics data enhances PPI prediction compared to using RNA or protein expression alone.

Notably, the model trained with protein expression (IHC data) achieved an AUROC of 0.9057, surpassing the RNA-only model (AUROC = 0.8837). This suggests that protein expression features provide a stronger signal for identifying functional interactions. Meanwhile, the RNA-only model still showed competitive performance, reinforcing the value of transcriptomic data in PPI prediction.

Training Dynamics

Throughout training, the model exhibited stable convergence patterns, with both AUROC and AP improving progressively across epochs (Figure 1). The final multi-omics model outperformed the RNA-only and protein-only configurations, underscoring the benefits of integrating complementary molecular data.
